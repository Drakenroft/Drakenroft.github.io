Unity as Game Engine

Unity is a cross-platform game engine developed by Unity Technologies (once called Over The Edge Entertainment), Unity is capable of making both Three-Dimensional and Two-Dimensional games, but it is not limited to game making, it is also widely used in the industry for CAD visualization, CGI-Film making and much more.

It was first introduced the June 8 of 2005, 12 yearso ago, and since then Unity has keep getting bigger util it has become the most used Game Development Tools ever. It has right now the biggest Game-Dev community with produce many Asset (both free and paid) to speed up the devlopment of your game, and the possability to sell the asset of a game,has saved many small teams and company because if they didn't reach the condition to sell the game on the market at least they can sell the asset to make some money and prevent to have to close.

Unity with color graduation

While company that sell asset of unfinished games are a reality, there are other few company that are directly focused on make asset to sell over make games, usually asset is not confined only to part of games that you can buy and put in your game to accellerate the devlopment (like 3d models or animation), usually they also offer many tools that extend Unity itself, to make an example ProBuilder is a famous tools, a plugin, that make possible to create 3D Models inside Unity, without have to download any other third party software. It is also offered free now, while once it was paid, this happen because when Unity Technology think that a plugin is really valuable it contact the creator to acquire the license, to give it free with the software to expand the possibility of the makers and have a more rich software to offer. Unity has three version, one of which is complitely free (until you have a annual gravenue less than 100.000$).

Unity integration with third party software

One of the main reason of why do many company and small team chose Unity is for high integration with well known third party software like Autodesk Maya, Blender, Substance Designer/Painter, Quixel Megascan which make Unity artistic and graphic possability near the photorealism.
There are some screenshoot of Unity working togheter with some famous software.

The most recent version of Unity has even introduced the possability to visualize and manipulate CAD and this has introduced many new user to Unity since even architects and engeener are using it now, to have a nice-looking render of their house/buildings or mechanical project like a moto or a car, to show to publisher how the final product should look.

Unity for Films

Games a part the second thing for which Unity is used a lot are films. Unity is awesome to make CGI films, even the Unity Technology itself has released 4 short films between this and the last year, with the raise of people using Unity for films it has been introducing many tools that can speed up and improve this process and the realization of those type of content.
